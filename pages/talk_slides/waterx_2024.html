Valence-limited models

  • Each atom/molecule/particle can form up to a maximum number of bonds
  • Interactions are (in most models) non-spherical
  • Useful to investigate the role of bonding in atomic and molecular systems[1,2]
  • Great model systems to design & realise new supramolecular materials[3]
[1] J. Russo et al., Rep. Prog. Phys. (2022), [2] F. Smallenburg, L. Filion and F. Sciortino, Nat. Phys. (2014)
[3] H. Liu et al., Science (2024)

The patchy model

  • Particles are spheres of diameter $\sigma$ decorated with $M = 4, 3$ or $2$ point patches
  • Fixed "optimal network" density $\rho \sigma^3 = 0.60$
  • A three-body potential enforces the single-bond-per-site constraint and enable a bond-swapping mechanism to speed-up the dynamics[1]
  • At low $T$ the patch-patch potential can be mapped on a square well of depth $\epsilon_{\rm eff}$
[1] F. Sciortino, Eur. Phys. J. E (2017), L. Rovigatti et al., Macromolecules (2018)

Why the swap?

  • There are systems where this is the only decorrelation channel (e.g. vitrimers[1])
  • Its relative importance w.r.t. thermal unbonding can be tuned in e.g. DNA systems [2]
  • Can be used to understand surprising dynamical effects (e.g. $q^0$ relaxation mode[3])
[1] W. Denissen, J. M. Winne, and Filip E. Du Prez, Chem. Sci. (2016)
[2] P. Å ulc et al. Biophys. J. (2015)
[3] L. Rovigatti et al., Macromolecules (2018), P. H. Handle et al., Phys. Rev. Lett. (2019)

Tetravalent ($M = 4$) particles

Structure

Large patches $\to$ high flexibility $\to$ no tetrahedral peak

Bonding is an activated process

  • A bond can be unambiguously defined
  • Arrhenius behaviour at low $T$
  • Exponents compatible with theoretical estimates of $\approx 0.5\, \epsilon_{\rm eff}$ [1]
[1] F. Smallenburg and F. Sciortino, Nat. Phys. (2013)

Diffusion constant & viscosity

  • From almost freely diffusive to supercooled
  • $D$ and $\eta$ are both Arrhenius at low $T$ $\to$ power laws if plotted against $X$
  • Swap vs. no-swap: same behaviour, different exponent

Rheology

  • Upon cooling $G'$ acquires a plateau $\to$ viscoelasticity
  • The frequency at which $G'$ and $G''$ cross correlates with $1 / \eta$ (not shown)
  • Without swap the elastic plateau does not depend on $T$
  • Bond-swapping $\to$ additional stress-relaxing channel: the plateau height decreases

Mixtures ($4 + 2$ and $3 + 2$)

The Stokes-Einstein relation

$$ \alpha_{\rm SE} = \frac{D\eta}{k_BT \rho^{1/3}} \approx 0.132 \left( 1 + \frac{\eta}{2\eta_l}\right) $$ where the second relation comes from R. Zwanzig, J. Chem. Phys. (1983)
  • $f_2$ is the fraction of patches belonging to divalent particles
  • Results are almost always compatible with Zwanzig's prediction
  • $\alpha_{\rm SE}$ increases at very low $T$ (as observed in supercooled molecular models [1,2])
[1] Y. J. Jung et al., Phys. Rev. E (2004), [2] S. R. Becker et al., Phys. Rev. Lett. (2006)

Power-law rheology

  • At low $T$, $G'$ and $G''$ of mixtures take on a power-law behaviour at intermediate $\omega$
  • Power-law rheology is a feature of many complex materials (see e.g. [1-3])
  • The exponent change continuously with the valence (see inset)
[1] B. Fabry et al., Phys. Rev. Lett. (2001), [2] K.-C. Hung et al, ,ACS Macro Lett. (2015), [3] N. Conrad et al., arXiv (2023)

Conclusions

  • Valence-limited systems: simple models for complex behaviour that keep on giving
  • Qualitative (and sometimes quantitative or semi-quantitative) agreement with experiments on "real" patchy particles
  • There is more: the $\rho$-dependence (unpublished since I don't understand it) is very rich
  • More details in "S. S. Gomez and L. Rovigatti, J. Chem. Phys. 160, 184901 (2024)"

Mapping to the a square well

The dimer partition function is defined as $$ Z_{ij} = 4 \pi \int_V e^{-V_{\rm pp}(R_{ij}, \Omega_i, \Omega_j) / k_B T} R_{ij}^2 dR_{ij} d\Omega_i d\Omega_j $$ where $V_{\rm pp}$ is the patch-patch interaction and $R_{ij}$, $\Omega_i$, $\Omega_j$ are the degrees of freedom.

The value of $\lambda$ controls the behaviour

  • $\lambda \geq 1$ $\to$ single-bond-per-patch
  • $\lambda = 1$ $\to$ free swapping!

How bonding controls $D$ and $\eta$

  • $f_2$ is the fraction of patches belonging to divalent particles
  • Mixtures can be mapped onto one-component effective systems
  • Bond-swapping $\to$ more complex $f_2$-dependence, all exponents are smaller (in absolute value)
